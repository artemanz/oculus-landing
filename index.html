Oculus controls are the handheld devices used to interact with the virtual world in Oculus VR games and experiences. They offer precise motion tracking and a variety of buttons, triggers, and joysticks to provide intuitive and immersive control over in-game actions and movements.

Set up your play area

The Oculus play area refers to the physical space in which a user can move and interact with the virtual environment in their Oculus VR headset. The size of the play area varies depending on the specific Oculus headset and the tracking technology used, but generally ranges from a small seated area to a larger room-scale space.

Gardian activity

Oculus Guardian is a built-in safety feature that helps users avoid collisions with real-world objects while using their VR headset. It allows users to map out their play area and set up virtual boundaries and obstacles that appear in-game when they approach the physical boundaries of their play area, ensuring a safe and enjoyable VR experience.

Headset casting

Oculus headset casting is a feature that allows users to share their VR experience with others on a nearby smartphone, tablet, or TV. It provides a real-time view of what the user is seeing and doing in the VR environment, making it possible for others to watch and even participate in the experience.
